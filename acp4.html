How to make pizza

Core Ingredients Dough: 1 lb (approx. 450-550g) of pizza dough, homemade or store-bought.

Sauce: 1/3 to 1/2 cup of pizza sauce or marinara.

Cheese: 1.5 to 2 cups of shredded low-moisture mozzarella. For more depth, add 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan or Romano.

Garnish: Dried oregano, garlic powder, or fresh basil leaves.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Prep the Oven: Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually 475°F to 500°F). If using a pizza stone or steel, place it on the center rack and let it heat for at least 30–45 minutes.

Shape the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, stretch or roll the dough into a 12 to 14-inch circle. Try to leave a slightly thicker border around the edge to form the crust.

Add Sauce and Cheese: Spread a thin, even layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border. Evenly sprinkle the mozzarella cheese over the sauce, followed by a dusting of Parmesan and any dried herbs like oregano.

Bake: Transfer the pizza to the preheated stone or a baking sheet. Bake for 8–14 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and starting to brown in spots.

Cool and Serve: Let the pizza rest for 2–5 minutes before slicing. This allows the cheese to set so it doesn't slide off when cut.

Expert Tips for Better Pizza Cheese Choice: While mozzarella is the "gold standard," blending it with Provolone, Fontina, or Cheddar can improve the melt and flavor profile.

Crispy Crust: Brushing the outer edge of the dough with olive oil before baking helps create a beautiful golden-brown, crispy finish.

Avoid Sogginess: Don't overdo the sauce; a thin layer is all you need to keep the crust from becoming soggy under the weight of the cheese.
